Bengal Tiger

The Bengal tiger is the most numerous tiger subspecies and is found primarily in India. These magnificent predators are known for their striking orange coat with black stripes, powerful build, and keen hunting abilities.

Interesting Facts:

  • Bengal tigers are the largest wild cats in the world, reaching up to 10 feet in length.
  • They are excellent swimmers and often cool off in water to escape the heat.
  • Bengal tigers are solitary animals and maintain large territories to ensure ample food supply.

Habitat:

Bengal tigers inhabit diverse habitats, including tropical rainforests, grasslands, and mangrove swamps. They require dense vegetation and access to water to thrive.

Behavior:

Bengal tigers are primarily nocturnal and rely on stealth and strength to hunt prey. They use their powerful jaws and sharp teeth to take down animals much larger than themselves.
