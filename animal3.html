King Cobra

The king cobra is the world's longest venomous snake, capable of reaching up to 18 feet in length. Known for its fearsome reputation and impressive hood display, this snake is highly respected and feared in many cultures.

Interesting Facts:

  • Despite its name, the king cobra is not a true cobra but belongs to its own genus, Ophiophagus, which means "snake-eater."
  • It preys primarily on other snakes, including pythons and even other venomous species.
  • When threatened, the king cobra can raise up to a third of its body off the ground and look a person in the eye.

Habitat:

King cobras are found in forests, bamboo thickets, and mangrove swamps across Southeast Asia. They prefer dense undergrowth and areas near water sources.

Behavior:

Unlike most snakes, king cobras are known for building nests for their eggs. The female guards the nest fiercely until the eggs hatch. King cobras are shy and will avoid confrontation if possible, but they can be aggressive when cornered.
