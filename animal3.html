King Cobra

The King Cobra is the world's longest venomous snake, capable of reaching lengths up to 18 feet. Known for its fearsome reputation and impressive hood display, this snake is highly respected and feared in many cultures.

Unlike most snakes, the King Cobra builds nests for its eggs and guards them fiercely until they hatch. It is capable of delivering a large amount of potent venom, which can be fatal to humans and large animals alike.

Interesting Facts:

  • The King Cobra's scientific name, Ophiophagus hannah, means "snake-eater," as it preys primarily on other snakes.
  • It can raise up to a third of its body off the ground and look a person in the eye.
  • Despite its name, the King Cobra is not a true cobra and belongs to its own genus.
  • King Cobras are excellent swimmers and often take refuge in water to escape danger.
  • King Cobras use their forked tongues to sense chemical signals in the environment, helping them track prey.
  • They can live up to 20 years in the wild, making them one of the longest-lived snakes.
  • King Cobras are highly intelligent and often avoid human contact unless threatened.

Habitat:

King Cobras are found in forests, bamboo thickets, and mangrove swamps across Southeast Asia. They prefer dense undergrowth and areas near water sources. They are often seen in India, Indonesia, and the Philippines.

Diet

Prey Description
Snakes Main prey, including both venomous and non-venomous species.
Lizards Occasionally hunted, especially in times of scarcity.
Rodents Hunted when other prey is not available.
Birds Rarely hunted, usually when other prey is scarce.

Conservation Efforts

The King Cobra is listed as vulnerable due to habitat destruction and hunting. Conservation organizations are working to protect these incredible snakes and their habitats.
